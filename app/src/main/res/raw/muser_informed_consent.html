Project ID # 000752

Researchers at the University of South Florida (USF) study many topics. To do this, we need the help of people who agree to take part in research studies. The person in charge of this research study is Dr. Hongdao Meng, Director of the Positive Aging Lab and Associate Professor in the School of Aging Studies at the University of South Florida.

Purpose of the Study

This is a unique opportunity to pilot test a music app designed to promote music engagement among persons with dementia and their caregivers. The purpose of this study is to gauge the feasibility and acceptability of the MUSER app for home use. The study should also measure whether music listening helps reduce agitation among persons living with dementia.

How do I participate?

You will be asked to answer questions regarding the music preferences of the person you are caring for, as well as yourself. You will then be asked to use an Android tablet preloaded with the MUSER app and a diverse collection of music. Next, you will be asked to use the app to listen to music for two weeks. Following this testing period, you will be asked to complete an exit survey and an interview about your experience using the app and the effect on the person living with dementia. Withdrawal

You can choose not to participate in this research study by tapping the "Disagree" button at the bottom of this form. If you wish to participate, you are free to withdraw at any time by uninstalling the app. There will be no penalty or loss of benefits if you stop taking part in this study.

Compensation, Benefits and Risks

There is no cost to participate. There is no compensation for participating in this study. We are not aware of any risk that would be associated with music listening at home.

Privacy and Confidentiality

We will store your data securely in a database accessible only to the research team. Your email address will be used for communication about this study (e.g., sending you a link to online surveys before and after the testing period) and will not be used for any other purpose.

We may publish and share what we learn from this study. If we do, we will not let anyone know your name. We will not share anything else that would allow people to know who you are.

Potential Conflict of Interest

Dr. Hongdao Meng and Dr. Sean Barbeau are the authors of the software application. Research studies like this one are done to determine whether the new software is effective. If research shows the new software is effective, Dr. Meng and Dr. Barbeau could receive a part of the profits from any sales of this software.

The Institutional Review Board that reviewed this study and a committee at the University of South Florida have reviewed the possibility of financial benefit. They believe that the possible financial benefit to Dr. Meng and Dr. Barbeau is not likely to affect your safety and/or the scientific quality of the study. If you would like more information, please ask the researchers or the study coordinator.
